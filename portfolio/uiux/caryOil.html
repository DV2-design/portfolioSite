Cary Oil Dashboards

Cary Oil, a prominent gas station supplier in the Southeast, wanted to restyle their existing dashboards and begin development of their brand's visual language as they expand markets.

The new dashboard uses ample whitespace to keep components distinct and standardized widget sizes to aid in navigation, decreasing visual fatigue.

Key Project Skills/Components

  • UI/UX
  • CSS
  • Sketch
  • Sencha

Originally, I was brought onto the project to update their system's UI to bring it in line with other modern applications. However, while meeting wih the company co-founder and the development team lead, I proposed that it was a missed opportunity to update the UI without consideration for the UX. We decided to explore ways to positively impact the most frequent users of the environment, their employees.

I started the discovery process by interviewing employees who interfaced with the application most often. I gathered feedback from them regarding how they use the system, things they enjoy about the current experience as well as their grievances, and determined what aspects of the system were working and what areas could be improved.

The most common responses were that the navigation was easy and straight forward (except for some behavioral frustrations), and that information on the page was difficult to track down and read. I put together a few different solutions at varying scales of implementation for management to review.

The final version, which maintained the core navigation while creating a clear visual hierarchy for the content, would be easy to implement quickly and could act as a trial run for future UI treatments in upcoming customer facing products. PIX, the first such product, touches on customizable dashboards and mobile environments.

Based on the previous internal dashboard, this customer facing tool focuses on quick status updates and recent activity.

Slightly exaggerated weights are used to increase clarity at a glance and aid in quick navigation.
